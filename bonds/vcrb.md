
### yield is a function of the NAV not the market price

The 30-day SEC yield for the Vanguard Core Bond ETF (VCRB) is a function of the net asset value (NAV). It is calculated by taking the net investment income earned by the fund's portfolio holdings (minus expenses) over the past 30 days and dividing it by the average NAV.

- Basis: The yield is based on the underlying portfolio value (NAV), not the market price, ensuring it reflects the income the bonds are generating.

### vcrb nav vs market price

The Net Asset Value (NAV) is the intrinsic, calculated value of a fund's underlying assets (minus liabilities) per share, typically updated once daily. The market price is the price at which you can buy or sell shares on an exchange throughout the day, driven by supply and demand. \[[1](https://www.investopedia.com/ask/answers/052815/what-difference-between-etfs-net-asset-value-nav-and-its-market-price.asp), [2](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/Wa5yxZ9gth8), [3](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/smart-money/what-is-nav)\]

Here is a breakdown of the differences:

* Calculation and Timing:  
  * NAV: Calculated once per day (usually after market close) as (Total Assets \- Liabilities) / Outstanding Shares.  
  * Market Price: Fluctuates continuously during trading hours based on buyer and seller activity.  
* Premium and Discount:  
  * Premium: When market price is higher than the NAV.  
  * Discount: When market price is lower than the NAV.  
* Usage:  
  * NAV: Crucial for evaluating the true worth of a fund's holdings, particularly for mutual funds and for calculating the cost basis for ETF transactions.  
  * Market Price: The price you actually pay or receive when buying or selling.  
* Arbitrage: For ETFs,, authorized participants (APs) can create/redeem shares when the market price differs significantly from the NAV, typically keeping the two prices close together. \[[1](https://www.chase.com/personal/investments/learning-and-insights/article/what-is-net-asset-value), [2](https://intrinio.com/blog/how-is-the-price-of-an-etf-determined), [3](https://www.investopedia.com/ask/answers/052815/what-difference-between-etfs-net-asset-value-nav-and-its-market-price.asp), [4](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/smart-money/what-is-nav), [5](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/Wa5yxZ9gth8)\]

*Note: For mutual funds, transactions occur at the NAV, not the market price, because they do not trade on an exchange.* \[[1](https://app.achievable.me/study/finra-series-66/learn/investment-vehicle-characteristics-mutual-funds-transactions)\]
