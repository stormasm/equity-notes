

### vcrb versus vpls

The primary difference between the Vanguard Core-Plus Bond ETF (VPLS) and Vanguard Core Bond ETF (VCRB) is risk exposure and flexibility: VPLS is a "core-plus" fund with higher risk tolerance (including high-yield/emerging markets) and a higher expense ratio (0.20%), while VCRB is a "core" fund focused on higher-quality investment-grade bonds with a lower expense ratio (0.10%).

Key Differences and Usage Examples:

Investment Strategy & Risk:

- VCRB (Core): Focuses on U.S. investment-grade securities, Treasuries, and agency mortgage-backed securities, targeting a higher-quality portfolio.
- VPLS (Core-Plus): Offers similar core exposure but has more flexibility to add higher allocations to riskier sectors, including U.S. high-yield corporates and emerging market debt.

Costs (Expense Ratios): VCRB is cheaper at 0.10%, while VPLS is 0.20%.

Performance Targets: VPLS targets an annual alpha of 75 basis points, while VCRB targets 60 basis points over their respective benchmarks.

Usage Examples:

- VCRB is best for investors seeking a foundation for their fixed-income portfolio with a high-quality bias and lower fees.
- VPLS is suited for investors seeking higher potential returns through active management, who are comfortable with higher risks.

Synonyms/Related Terms:

- VCRB: Vanguard Core Bond ETF, Core Bond Portfolio, Investment-Grade Bond ETF.
- VPLS: Vanguard Core-Plus Bond ETF, Core-Plus Fund, Actively Managed Bond ETF.

Note:

Both funds were launched in December 2023 and are actively managed, meaning they aim to outperform their respective benchmarks rather than just track them
